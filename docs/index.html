Abstract

Generative speech enhancement based on Conditional Flow Matching (CFM) offers efficient deterministic sampling with high output quality. However, two bottlenecks limit its practical performance: heterogeneous training data quality that degrades the learned velocity field, and training instability when applying reinforcement learning (RL) alignment to the continuous mel-spectrogram domain.

This work presents a systematic post-training framework that addresses both issues. On the data side, we design a DNSMOS-based hierarchical scoring and filtering pipeline that constructs three tiered training subsets (18,158h / 327h / 3.18h) from 56,700 hours of open-source speech. On the algorithm side, we adopt GRPO-GUARD to stabilize on-policy RL alignment for flow matching, incorporating RatioNorm, gradient balancing, and MSE-based KL divergence. The resulting system achieves DNSMOS OVRL 3.415 on the DNS 2020 No Reverb test set with an RTF of 0.066, outperforming all compared baselines while running over 50× faster than diffusion-based alternatives.

Method Overview

Our framework follows a three-stage PT-SFT-RL pipeline applied to a 336M-parameter Conditional Flow Matching model with a DiT backbone.

1. Hierarchical Data Curation

We score all 56,700 hours of aggregated clean speech corpora using a segmented DNSMOS evaluation strategy, then apply tiered filtering based on SIG and BAK sub-scores to construct three subsets: PT (18,158h, absolute thresholds), SFT (327h, top 10%), and RL (3.18h, top 0.5%).

2. Supervised Fine-Tuning

The base FlowSE model is fine-tuned on the 327-hour high-quality subset with additive noise augmentation only (no reverberation), achieving OVRL +0.170 over the base model in 66,000 steps.

3. GRPO-GUARD Alignment

Starting from the SFT checkpoint, we apply LoRA adapters (1.57M parameters) and train with on-policy GRPO using DNSMOS OVRL as the reward. GRPO-GUARD stabilizes training through RatioNorm, gradient balancing, and MSE-based KL divergence, preventing the reward hacking observed in vanilla Flow-GRPO.
